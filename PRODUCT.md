# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Dog owners ("lead humans") and their co-caregivers — partners, family, walkers, foster carers — coordinating daily dog care across a shared household or care network. Lead humans set schedules and assign tasks; co-caregivers log activity and view the shared record. Users are in domestic/outdoors contexts: checking the app quickly between walks, logging an event from the couch, or reviewing yesterday's feeding before leaving for work.

## Product Purpose

PackOps is multi-human dog care coordination. It lets a care team log routines, schedule future tasks, track medical records, and stay in sync — so nothing falls through the cracks and the dog is always cared for. Success looks like: no duplicated feedings, no missed medications, and every human knowing exactly what's happened and what's next.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, practical, warm. The "Field Station" aesthetic: competent and reliable without being cold; human without being cutesy. The app feels like a well-worn tool a serious dog person actually reaches for, not a consumer toy or a clinical health tracker.

## Anti-references

- **Generic pet apps (BarkBox, Rover)**: puppy paw icons, cartoon dogs, pastel playfulness, juvenile tone. PackOps serves people who take dog care seriously.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: blue/grey enterprise grids, cold color palettes, zero personality. This is a personal tool, not a B2B product.
- **Consumer social apps (Facebook, Instagram)**: story tiles, engagement-bait patterns, rounded-everything aesthetic. No social feed metaphors.

## Design Principles

1. **Log fast, read faster.** Every logging interaction should feel instant. Viewing history should feel like scanning a field journal, not filling out a form.
2. **Role clarity without ceremony.** Lead humans have more power — surface that power naturally in context, not through gates and warnings.
3. **The dog is the center.** UI always references which dog you're looking at. Multi-dog households switch dogs, not "accounts."
4. **Dense without feeling crowded.** Care logs are inherently list-heavy. Use rhythm, weight, and spacing to make density feel organized, not overwhelming.
5. **Trust the user.** No confirmation dialogs for common actions. Undo beats "Are you sure?".

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. All interactive elements minimum 44×44px touch targets. No color-only state indicators. Reduced-motion support for animations.
